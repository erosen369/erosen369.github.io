About Me


Hello, my name is Ethan Rosenbaum. I work as a professional software engineer within a BIOS firmware development group. All development for this codebase is done in C and all of the work is on embedded platforms. There are some other programming languages used, BaSH, Ruby, Python, for automation and testing.


I grew up in Andover, MA. I currently live in Lowell, MA, with my cat, Barry. Hes very large, very grey, and very sweet. He enjoys sitting in windows, rolling around on the floor, and sleeping.


My primary hobby is athletics. I have completed at least one competition or competitive season in basketball, marathon canoe racing, gymnastics, ultimate frisbee, and soccer. In the summers, I enjoy open water swimming and trail running in the summer. Currently, I am taking parkour classes at Hub Parkour Training Center in Norton MA.


A secondary hobby of mine is reading. I prefer non-fiction, but I do love a good story and have been known to read historical fiction. A Gentleman in Moscow by Amore Towles and The Inner Game of Tennis by W. Timothy Gallway are my favorite fiction and non-fiction books of this recent part of my life. Presently, I am researching chronic disease, specifically as it relates to diet, as well as proper human biomechanics for running and walking. This site contains a table of books which I have read recently, links to the author's website, and a brief review of each text.

Recently Read


Book Author Completed Comments
Katy Bowman 04/30/2023

A collection of essays by biomechanist Katy Bowman. It delves into the minutia of movement and extrapolates the consequences of a sedentary lifestyle on the human body and the world we live in. I found the perspective of nutritious movement to be compelling. Nutritious movement supports and improves ones capability to inhabit positions, walk up hills, run without pain, etc. It made me re-think the natural quality of what I do in the gym and reconsider my goals for how I can move.

Nina Teicholz 03/23/2023

Science reporter Nina Teicholz presents a compelling case for including saturated fat in a healthy diet. Her well cited work presents the history of the medical establishments war on fat, takes apart the data used to justify the decisions to recommend limitations on fat to americans, and exposes the weak evidence for the 'healthy' diets sold to us, such as the low fat and mediterranean diets.

Benjamin Bikman 03/20/2023

An excellent description of the hormonal processes and impacts of chronic disease. Ben Bikman's writing is insightful and digestable. This book is empowering for someone who wants to have a better handle on the care they need when dealing with their condition.
